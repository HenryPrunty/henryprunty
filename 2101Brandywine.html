2101 Brandywine St.

Instructor: Patrick Till — Spring 2026

This project was the adaptive reuse of an existing industrial building in the Fairmount neighborhood of Philadelphia. The clients were two non-profits, Treehouse Books and Mighty Writers, who focus on providing literacy tools for the Philadelphia community.

This building would serve as the headquarters of the two non-profits, providing afterschool classrooms on the bottom floor for Mighty Writers and a small community library on the middle floor operated by treehouse books. On the top floor there would be private study rooms as well as a terrace, both of which would be open for the public.

Connecting the different floors is a large staircase which extends up past the roof to collect light, shooting it back down to all three floors. This staircase acts as a connector through both circulation and light allowing the differing programs on each floor to interact.
